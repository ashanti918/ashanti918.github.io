The Acacia Trees

Main Page

Most acacia tree types are fast growers, but they usually live only 20 to 30 years. Many varieties are valued for their long roots which help stabilize the soil in areas threatened by erosion

Acacia trees were founded in africa. they provide food for the animals. they protect themselves in many ways. the trees protect themselves First, most species have long, sharp thorns, which prevent (most) animals from eating their leaves. Second, sometimes stinging ants live inside hollowed-out thorns, which provides another disincentive for predators.

The Trees have their own shape, they are dome shaped to catch the maximum amount of sunlight , with the smallest of leaves. The wood of the tree is special because it is resistant to decay because the tree deposits in the heartwood many waste substances.

The leaves of acacias send a warning to other plants that herbivores are feeding by releasing ethylene gas thats how they warn each other of danger. The acacia tree has some unique adaptations to protect itself from animals. One such adaptation is the poisonous alkaloid that the tree pumps into the leaves. This discourages the animals from eating the leaves
